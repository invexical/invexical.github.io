Problem 3

Category: Algebra, Geometry, and Combinatorics

How many isosceles triangles are there with positive area whose side lengths are all positive integers and whose longest side has length $2025$? $$\textbf{(A) } 2025 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 2026 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 3012 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 3037 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 4050$$

Difficulty: 8/100 — This problem is quite easy to approach, however, the casework is a little challenging. The contestant must realize there are two cases.

Core Concepts: The contestant should know how to set up two cases for when the side length of $2025$ is the repeated side or the side opposite to the vertex. Additionally, they must know how to find how many such triangles are satisfactory for each case.

Non-obvious Transitions: Knowing how to deal with the transition from geometry to a combinatoric interpretation.

Techniques: The contestant should know how to establish an inequality and solve it for all possible cases, accounting for overlap between the cases.

Error-prone Steps: Although not an answer choice, it is easy to forget or double-count the equilateral triangle case.

Ideal Time: Experienced contestants may take up to $25$ seconds to complete the problem. Intermediate contestants may take up to $45$ seconds to complete the problem. Beginner contestants may take up to $1.5$ minutes to solve the problem.
