Problem 7

Category: Algebra

Suppose $a$ and $b$ are real numbers. When the polynomial $x^3+x^2+ax+b$ is divided by $x-1$, the remainder is $4$. When the polynomial is divided by $x-2$, the remainder is $6$. What is $b-a$? $$\textbf{(A) } 14 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 15 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 16 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 17 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 18$$

Difficulty: 18/100 — Purely conceptual, borderline bashy if you don't know the theorem.

Core Concepts:The remainder theorem is required to solve this problem; any other way is quite tedious.

Non-obvious Transitions: For an inexperienced contestant who may not be familiar with the remainder theorem, this problem is truly nothing more than a confusion bubble.

Techniques: Solve a system of equations once the remainder theorem is applied.

Error-prone Steps: Misreading the problem (mixing up the linear expressions).

Ideal Time: Experienced contestants may take up to $30$ seconds to complete the problem. Intermediate contestants may take up to $40$ seconds to complete the problem. Beginner contestants may take up to $1-1.5$ minutes to solve the problem.
