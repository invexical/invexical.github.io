In $\triangle ABC $, $AB=10$, $AC=18$, and $\angle B=130^\circ.$ Let $O$ be the center of the circle containing $A$, $B$, and $C.$ What is the degree measure of $\angle CAO$?

$$\textbf{(A) } 20 \qquad \textbf{(B) } 30 \qquad \textbf{(C) } 40 \qquad \textbf{(D) } 50 \qquad \textbf{(E) } 60$$
Difficulty: 31/100 — Relient on knowledge of inscribed angle theorem, and somewhat confusing.
Core Concepts: circles, inscribed angles
Challenges: If the contestant is unfamiliar with circle concepts such as inscribed angle theorem, the problem is almost impossible.
Techniques: Creating a point opposite to $B$ and utilizing inscribed angle and isosceles triangles.
Error-prone Steps: Assuming $O$ is in the triangle, arithmetic errors, applying theorems incorrectly
Ideal Time:
Experienced: ≤ 30 sec
Intermediate: ≤ 30-90 sec
Beginner: ≤ 3 min
